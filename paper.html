01A photon arrives poorer than it left

Light from the early universe reaches us today carrying far less energy than it set out with. A particle of light that left an atom long ago arrives now stretched out, redder, weaker. Multiply that across all the light crossing an expanding cosmos and a natural question forms: where did all that energy go?

The answer, standard in physics for about a century, is blunt: nowhere — because in an expanding universe there is no global energy that was ever required to be conserved. That sounds like a dodge. It is not. It is a precise statement about when the conservation of energy is even guaranteed, and an fails the condition. This dossier shows that one result three independent ways, and lets you run the numbers.

"Energy must be conserved" is not a law of nature everywhere — it is a theorem with a hypothesis, and the expanding universe does not meet the hypothesis.

02Leg 1 — No symmetry, no conserved energy

In general relativity the local law is exact: the obeys , ∇µTµν=0 . But that is a local bookkeeping identity, not a global one. To get a genuinely conserved total energy — a single number that never changes — you need the spacetime to have a time-translation symmetry: a . An expanding FRW universe has none — it is genuinely different at each epoch — so there is no conserved global energy to begin with.

You also cannot rescue the books by claiming the lost energy hides in the gravitational field: in general relativity the energy of the gravitational field cannot be localized at all . There is simply no global ledger. This is textbook, not fringe . [established-reproduced — conceptual leg; see refs]

Go deeper: why covariant ≠ globally conserved
A conserved charge needs an ordinary (partial) divergence that integrates by Gauss's theorem. From a Killing field ξ one builds a current and recovers that:
J^a = T^{ab} ξ_b , ∇_a J^a = (∇_a T^{ab}) ξ_b + T^{ab} ∇_a ξ_b = 0
The first term vanishes by ∇_aT^{ab}=0; the second by Killing's equation ∇_(a ξ_b)=0. So ∇_aJ^a=0 becomes an ordinary conservation law — but only because ξ exists. In FRW there is no timelike ξ, so this construction is unavailable.

Conservation of energy is the shadow of a symmetry. Remove the symmetry and the shadow goes with it.

03Leg 2 — The bookkeeping: radiation falls as 1/a

Now watch the energy directly. In an FRW universe the a sets how everything dilutes. The continuity equation gives a clean rule: the energy density of any scales as ρ ∝ a−3(1+w) .

  • Matter (w=0): ρ ∝ a−3 checkable · verify_numbers.py N02 — pure dilution, the same stuff spread through a volume that grew as a³.
  • Radiation (w=1/3): ρ ∝ a−4 checkable · verify_numbers.py N01 — one extra factor of 1/a beyond dilution.

Where does the extra 1/a come from? The number of photons in a box dilutes exactly like matter, as a−3; but each individual photon also loses energy as 1/a as it redshifts . So count the total radiation energy inside a fixed (whose physical size grows as a³):

Erad = ρrad × V ∝ a−4 × a3 = a−1.

The total radiation energy in a region that always contains the same photons falls as 1/a checkable · verify_numbers.py N03. As the universe doubles in size, that energy halves. There is no term anywhere in the equations that catches it. It is not transferred; it is not stored; it is simply not conserved. [established-reproduced]

Go deeper: the continuity equation and its solution
The ν=0 component of ∇µTµ0=0 in FRW is
ρ̇/ρ = −3 (ȧ/a)(1 + w) ⇒ ρ ∝ a^(−3(1+w))
For radiation, tracelessness of the electromagnetic stress-energy forces w=1/3, giving ρ∝a⁻⁴; for dust w=0 gives ρ∝a⁻³. The decomposition a⁻⁴ = a⁻³ (number) × a⁻¹ (per-photon redshift) is checked explicitly. checkable · N05

Same photons, same box — and a steadily shrinking pile of energy. The deficit is the result.

04Leg 3 — The concrete face: cosmological redshift

Leg 2's "each photon loses energy as 1/a" has a face you can put a number on. The oldest light we can see is the cosmic microwave background (CMB) — radiation released when the universe first turned transparent, at . A photon's energy scales as E ∝ 1/a, so the energy it has now versus when it left is

Eobs / Eemit = 1 / (1 + z*) ≈ 9.17 × 10−4.

Each CMB photon arrives with about 0.09% of the energy it was born with checkable · verify_numbers.py N04. The other ~99.9% is gone. [established-reproduced]

HONEST SEAMSo where did the 99.9% go? Nowhere — and that is the whole point, not a paradox. "Lost energy must reappear somewhere" is a theorem of physics with time-translation symmetry (Leg 1); an expanding universe has none, so there is no global ledger for it to enter. Three honest caveats this worked number leans on, stated rather than waved through: (1) it is the per-photon redshift factor only — the number-dilution of Leg 2 is a separate effect; (2) "energy" here is the photon energy as measured by comoving observers, a frame choice; (3) the value depends on the stated z*. Change any of these and the number changes — which is exactly why it is a checkable claim (N04), not a slogan.

The redshift you can measure tonight is the conservation law failing, in the open, with a number attached.
